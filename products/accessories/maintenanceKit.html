10-Minute Maintenance

  1. Shut & Drain: Close spigot, remove upper chamber/filters, and empty lower chamber.
  2. Inspect Seals: Check spigot and chamber gaskets for flat spots or cracks.
  3. Swap Parts: Replace gaskets and any worn stainless washers; reassemble hand-tight + 1/4 turn.
  4. Clean Surfaces: Use pad/brush with mild soap; rinse thoroughly.
  5. Prime Filters: Use priming aid (if included) and reinstall filters per guide.
  6. Leak-Check: Fill upper chamber, open spigot, and confirm a dry exterior after 5 minutes.
Tip: Avoid harsh abrasives or chlorine bleach on stainless; they can dull the finish. Re-check nut tension after first fill.

All you need to know about keeping your BOROUX system in peak shape with the Maintenance Kit.

What’s included in the Maintenance Kit?
The kit includes: a blue priming tool, two replacement hole plugs, two replacement wingnuts, two replacement washers, two vials of red food dye (for testing filter efficiency), and a Scotch-Brite scrubbing pad for cleaning your system.

How often should I use the Maintenance Kit?
BOROUX recommends cleaning the stainless steel chambers and spigot monthly. The Foundation Filters should be scrubbed every 3-6 months or when you notice a slow flow rate. The Maintenance Kit supplies the tools to carry out that maintenance.
Source: BOROUX “How to Clean” guide. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
Can the red dye vials actually test my filter?
Yes — the red food dye included helps test whether your filters are still sealing properly and filtering as expected. If the dye passes through quickly, it's a sign it’s time for deeper maintenance or replacement.
