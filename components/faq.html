Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about how the Foundation™ filters work, what they’re made to remove, and how long they last.

How do the BOROUX Foundation™ Filters work?
They use a specialized activated-carbon block to both adsorb contaminants (they stick to the carbon’s surface) and physically trap tiny particles in microscopic pores—like a super-fine sieve. This combination targets taste/odor compounds and a wide range of chemical contaminants.
Source: BOROUX FAQ & product details.
What certifications do the Foundation™ Filters have?
They are certified by the Water Quality Association (WQA) to NSF/ANSI 42, NSF/ANSI/CAN 401, and NSF/ANSI/CAN 372.
Source: BOROUX product FAQ.
How long do the filters last?
The recommended replacement interval is every 12 months under typical household use. Actual life varies with water quality and daily volume; replacing annually keeps performance within verified reduction levels.
Source: BOROUX blog guidance on lifespan.
What contaminants are they designed to reduce?
Independent testing shows broad reduction across categories such as disinfectants/byproducts, heavy metals, PFAS, pharmaceuticals, VOCs/industrial chemicals, pesticides, and micro-particles. See the full test panel and results for specific compounds and reduction percentages.
Source: “What Does BOROUX Filter?” page with verified third-party results.
Do the Foundation™ Filters reduce fluoride?
No—fluoride reduction is provided by the BOROUX Proactive™ Fluoride Filters, which install as a second stage.
Source: BOROUX FAQ.
Are Foundation™ Filters compatible with other gravity systems?
Yes—Foundation™ Filters are designed to fit most common gravity-fed systems. Check the compatibility list if you’re adapting an existing unit.
Source: BOROUX FAQ.
Where are the filters made?
The filters are proudly crafted in the USA. (Accessory components such as the plastic Stem Saver™ may be sourced internationally.)
Source: BOROUX FAQ.
What’s the micron performance?
Guidance from BOROUX notes effective capture in the ~0.5–1.0 μm range for many waterborne particles—small enough to reduce micro-particulates while maintaining flow.
Source: BOROUX micron-rating article.
How do I prime or set up the filters?
Follow the step-by-step priming and setup instructions provided in BOROUX’s How-To guides for best flow and performance.
Source: BOROUX How-To pages (priming & setup).
