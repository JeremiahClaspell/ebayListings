Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about how the Foundation™ filters work, what they’re made to remove, and how long they last.

How do the BOROUX Foundation™ Filters work?
They use a specialized activated-carbon block to both adsorb contaminants and physically trap tiny particles in microscopic pores—like a super-fine sieve. This combination targets taste/odor compounds and a wide range of chemical contaminants.
Source: BOROUX FAQ & product details.
What certifications do the Foundation™ Filters have?
Certified by the Water Quality Association (WQA) to NSF/ANSI 42, NSF/ANSI/CAN 401, and NSF/ANSI/CAN 372.
Source: BOROUX product FAQ.
How long do the filters last?
Recommended replacement is every 12 months under typical household use. Actual life varies with water quality and daily volume.
Source: BOROUX blog guidance on lifespan.
What contaminants are they designed to reduce?
Broad reduction across disinfectants/byproducts, heavy metals, PFAS, pharmaceuticals, VOCs/industrial chemicals, pesticides, and micro-particles. See full test results for specific compounds and reduction percentages.
Source: “What Does BOROUX Filter?” page.
Do the Foundation™ Filters reduce fluoride?
No—fluoride reduction is provided by the BOROUX Proactive™ Fluoride Filters as a second stage.
Source: BOROUX FAQ.
Are Foundation™ Filters compatible with other gravity systems?
Yes—designed to fit most common gravity-fed systems. Check the compatibility list if adapting an existing unit.
Source: BOROUX FAQ.
Where are the filters made?
Filters are crafted in the USA. (Some accessory components may be sourced internationally.)
Source: BOROUX FAQ.
What’s the micron performance?
Effective capture in the ~0.5–1.0 μm range for many waterborne particles—small enough to reduce micro-particulates while maintaining flow.
Source: BOROUX micron-rating guidance.
How do I prime or set up the filters?
Follow BOROUX’s step-by-step How-To guides for priming and setup to ensure best flow and performance.
Source: BOROUX How-To pages.
